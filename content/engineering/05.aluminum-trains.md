# Aluminum Train Cabs

![Three aluminum train cabs](/cam/trains.jpg)

RPI believes that to be a good engineer, one must understand how their design will be realized.  All Mechanical Engineering students take Engineering Processes, an exploratory course with a semester project to make a train cab or a lightsaber handle out of aluminum using a vertical mill and lathe.  I chose to make the train cab, which was far more interesting (and difficult), and while there are several dimensions out of tolerance, it at least looks like a train (leftmost object in the photo above).  Three years later, I took the Computer Aided Machining course, where I learned how to program a CNC mill and lathe to make a more complex version of this train cab.  As a learning opportunity, one side of the cab is machined using handwritten G-code, while the other side, the boiler and smokestack, and central bore are machined using G-code generated by Mastercam.  Once inspected for dimensional accuracy, the finished train cab is sent to a sponsor to be anodized (rightmost object in the photo above).  Failed attempts (center object) are used as a reference when correcting the G-code.  Check out the drawings for the CAM train cab below (this model is reused for the course, so I cannot share my G-code)!
