# New Standard Corporation Internship

![shadowboard](/nsc/shadowboard.jpg)

[New Standard Corporation](https://newstandard.com) is a contract manufacturer that makes primarily automotive parts.  My internship focused on a number of projects and involved making drawings, visual work instructions, ERP system entries, getting quotes for tool purchases, overseeing shop floor operations, and designing "poka-yokes" to prevent operators from making mistakes.  Due to the nature of the contracts, much of my work cannot be discussed, however, there are three notable tasks that I can share.

## Shadowboard

The shadowboard pictured above was a "poka-yoke" for an assembly process that involved welding PEM studs and nuts to a plate.  The quality department at one of New Standard's facilities discovered a large lot of 720908 parts that were missing PEM studs or nuts, which was the sole responsibility of the operators at that station.  In response, I created this shadowboard to make it obvious that parts had been missed during the assembly.  For more information, check out the attached drawings and visual work instructions.

- [Visual Work Instructions](/nsc/VWI03938.pdf)
- [720908 Shadowboard Assembly Drawing](/nsc/720908-10-001-F-Prestage-Fixture.pdf)

## Poka-Yoke Fixture

![poka-yoke](/nsc/poka-yoke.jpg)

Another "poka-yoke" I made prevented weld nuts from being placed over the wrong hole.  New Standard made two parts that were identical except for the location of a single weld nut, which frequently confused operators.  In response, I designed a ["poka-yoke" gauge](/nsc/721001-721313-MT406-SP-Rev-A.pdf) with the corresponding part numbers engraved into either side, which operators can use to verify that they have made the correct part.

## Dot Matrix Fixture

![dot-matrix-fixture](/nsc/dot-matrix-fixture.jpg){.w-auto width="300"}

As New Standard borrowed tools, fixtures, and gauges from their customers, project engineers frequently needed to engrave asset tags with the customer's information.  This was a tedious process, however, one of the shop floor operators had a device that made this process easy.  I designed a fixture to hold the asset tags while this device was in use, which would guarantee the alignment of the text and prevent slippage.

- [Visual Work Instructions](/nsc/ENG-124-10-001-VWI.pdf)
