# Audio Animatronic

![Gizmo the Rockin' Robot album cover artwork](/animatronic/gizmo.jpg)

*Credit: Aarushi Kumar*

Inspired by Disney's theme park animatronic figures, the RPI Robotics Club started a beginner-friendly project to design, build, and program an animatronic mascot to greet visitors to the club's room with a song!  Over the course of a year, our team of artistic designers created "Gizmo, the Rockin' Robot" as our mascot, while our songwriting team wrote his theme song, our mechanical design team designed 5 joint mechanisms, and our electrical and programming team began integrating all of the joints with an Arduino and programming in Bottango, a previsualization software for joint movements.

## Mechanical Design

The mechanical design team was responsible for taking the concept designers' ideas and designing the mechanisms required to implement them.  I was responsible for the rotating torso mechanism, which uses an internal gear drive to rotate an outer shell using an SG-90 servo motor.

### Torso Mechanism

In the initial brainstorming meeting, the team decided that the figure's torso should be capable of rotating through a range of 180 degrees.  Since exposed mechanisms were not feasible for an animatronic figure, the obvious solution was an internal gear mechanism.  Some of the design constraints included the SG-90 motor, a conduit for wires to pass through, and the ability to support the figure's body and upper mechanisms.  The initial sketch of this mechanism, while messy, captured this idea from the beginning, with two options for mounting and gearing the motor.

![A concept sketch of Gizmo's torso mechanism](/animatronic/torso-concept.jpg){.w-auto width="200"}

Considering all parts were to be 3D printed, the only necessary design calculations were the gear ratio and tooth spacing of the outer gear.  The pinion was chosen from a collection of random gears and measured with calipers.  As this pinion had 30 teeth and a diametral pitch of about 50, the outer gear was designed with 188 evenly spaced teeth to ensure proper meshing with the pinion without changing its diameter (diametral pitches of gears must match for proper meshing).  The outer gear was also designed in two halves, connected by magnets, to allow easy assembly and serviceability.  The final version also incorporates slots to attach the legs and body frame.

## Lighting Design

No performance would be complete without elaborate lighting!  Coming soon, as the team finalizes their designs of Gizmo and all of the stage accessories, I will be programming a simulated light show in my model of the EMPAC Theatre using ETC Eos Augment3d!  The light show will be timecoded to Gizmo's song, and although his joints will not move, this will serve as a promotional video to introduce Gizmo to the RPI community!
