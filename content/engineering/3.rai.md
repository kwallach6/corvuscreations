# Robotic Art Installation

![The Robotic Art Installation project at a competition](/hero/rai.jpg)

The Robotic Art Installation is a unique blend between the arts and a highly complex machine with over 1500 components!  Shortly after the RPI Robotics Club was founded in 2022, the president of RPI asked the club to take on a project that blended robotics and the arts.  I joined this project in 2025 amidst its third iteration: a drawing machine that was as complex as possible.  The robot has four arms that can pivot, slide toward and away from the center, and drop a pen to trace its movement.  All four arms are positioned around a central canvas that spins around its central axis.  This totals to a whopping 5 rotational degrees of freedom, 4 linear degrees of freedom, and a "half" degree of freedom for each pen.  While this is quite excessive, once finished, this robot will not just create art, but watching it draw will be an art of its own!

## Spring 2025 Competition

As an initiative to boost the RPI Robotics Club's presence on campus, the art installation project was entered into the [2025 MassRobotics Form & Function Challenge](https://www.massrobotics.org/form-function-challenge/) held at the Robotics Summit and Expo in Boston, MA.  As such, the project was rushed to completion and almost completely undocumented.  Come competition day, the robot was fully assembled and power was distributed to the motors, however, there were no programs ready to run and no motion commands worked.

## Redesign and Documentation

Following the competition failure, I stepped into the unofficial roles of senior mechanical engineer and manufacturing manager for the project.  My initial proposal was to make necessary improvements to the design, such as rollers to prevent the canvas from sagging, and slowly document the project as we made changes.  This proved to be a problem, as the CAD assembly was so messy that it was impossible to track down individual components.  Additionally, unconstrained features and a lack of a bill of materials and drawings made the project impossible to even understand.  As such, I persuaded the team lead to pause all changes and help me document the project from scratch.

![The CAD assembly with the added rollers.](/rai/rai-rollers.png)

*Rollers added to the CAD assembly.  A lack of documentation would have made getting approval to machine the roller brackets impossible.*

### Rebuilding the Assembly

One of the biggest challenges with this project was the disorganized and unconstrained CAD assembly.  The full project is about 1500 parts, and if any one of them is improperly constrained or not organized, it becomes infinitely harder to understand how the robot works and how parts fit together.  Thus, the first task was reviewing all 141 unique components and ensuring that all sketches were fully constrained, no features were broken, and all subassemblies were properly constrained.  While the figure below looks nearly identical to the original, all parts are now properly constrained, making the full assembly much more robust.

![The redeveloped CAD assembly](/rai/rai-cad.png)

### Organizing the File System

Compounding the issues with the CAD assembly was the mess of a folder that all of the part files were stored in.  While there was a part numbering scheme in use, these part numbers were not consistently used as file names, especially for purchased parts, and three iterations of CAD files were scattered between folders, making it impossible to determine which files were outdated.  The new file structure has a folder for each part number with that part's CAD file, drawing, and associated simulations or information stored in it.  All CAD files also have the part number and name in their file properties, making it easy to identify them in the assembly.

![A sampling of the Art Installation CAD directory.](/rai/rai-files.png)

### Creating Documentation

During the competition preparations, one of the biggest challenges was manufacturing, not only because of the tight timeline, but mainly because of the lack of documentation needed for the manufacturing staff to make parts.  After finishing the CAD redevelopment, the team has dedicated ourselves to making drawings for each part and assembly, and having others review and approve each one.  Additionally, while reorganizing the CAD folder, I compiled a bill of materials and verified it against the bill of materials generated by Autodesk Inventor.  Check out a sample drawing and the bill of materials linked below!

## A Brief Overview of How The Robot Will Work

The Art Installation robot has 4 Novanta NEMA17 Integrated Steppers controlling the arm rotations, 1 Novanta NEMA23 Integrated Stepper controlling the canvas rotation, 4 generic stepper motors controlling the arm linear motion, and 4 electromagnets to drop the pens.  A Raspberry Pi 5 serves as the main computer and will process an uploaded image, apply inverse kinematics to it, and command the motors and electromagnets to draw the image.
