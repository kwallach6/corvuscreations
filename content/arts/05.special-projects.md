# RPI Players Special Projects

![The cast of the 2024 Pride Cabaret](/special-projects/IMG-9929.jpg)

*The cast of the 2024 Pride Cabaret.*

Special projects are productions, workshops, or any other RPI Players events that are not part of the regular season.  They tend to be smaller, less organized projects designed to provide easier access for those who may not be interested in a mainstage show.

## Arch After Hours

*Arch After Hours* was a sketch comedy show in August 2025, primarily targeted at the Summer Arch program and the student experience at RPI.  Each sketch was written by students, many of whom acted in the show.  I volunteered to assistant stage manage the show after the lighting team repeatedly expressed a need.  My duties included overseeing the props table, costumes rack, and backstage area, as well as calling entrances, setting and striking the stage, and managing the projector.  My first day was on the first day of tech week, and I had to step in for the stage manager and call the lighting and sound cues for the second and third tech runs.

## Pride Cabaret 2025

![pride-2025](/special-projects/pride-2025.jpg)

June 2025 marked the return of the Pride Cabaret at the annual Upper Hudson Queer Alliance Pride Picnic at Lake Luzerne.  This cabaret's theme was "pride through the ages", with a song representing LGBTQ+ progress during the decade it was released, spanning from the 1920s to 2020s.  Actors were encouraged to pick a song to audition with that would fit this theme, but if they did not, one was assigned for them by the director.  I was the musical director and stage manager for this production, and learned quickly that those two roles are not compatible.  Long story short, music direction is about continuous improvement, whereas stage management is about keeping things on schedule and well organized.  I spent most of my time arranging music and leading music rehearsals, and even recorded the accompaniment for one of the numbers.

## Improv! The Musical

In March 2024, the RPI Players produced a fully improvised musical!  This was an interesting idea, and it actually turned out quite well!  I stepped in at the last minute to help the lighting team that needed an extra person.  Things got interesting, as there were no prerecorded cues and the balcony wall in the Rensselaer Union was too tall to see over while sitting.  As such, the lighting operations became a game of telephone, where the lighting designer would watch the show, relay instructions to me to busk, and then both of us would relay instructions to the followspot operator.  To make things more interesting, some of the equipment that was provided by Union Show Techs was not working.  Check out the recording of this experiment and see for yourself!
