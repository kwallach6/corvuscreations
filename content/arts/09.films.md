# Film Projects

![detective1](/films/detective1.png)

I took 4 film production classes at RPI, where I made everything from satirical advertisements to a 20-minute detective film! These classes taught a variety of techniques and genres, including televised interviews, advertisements, newscasts, music videos, hybrid films, multi-camera shoots, green-screen, projection, and remixing.

## Behind the TV Screen

This was an introductory level class that is restricted to freshmen.  In addition to producing short video projects, this class covers the history of television and media.

::accordion{type="multiple"}
  :::accordion-item{label="Project 1: Interview"}
  The first project was simple, film a live interview in the studio using a 3-camera shoot.  My team decided to role-play an interview with an experimental artist, played by me, that sells blank canvases as art.  The interview quickly goes off the rails, as my answers become increasingly absurd, leading to an unexplained show of cat-like behavior.  Unfortunately, the final cut was lost after the class ended.
  :::

  :::accordion-item{label="Project 2: Advertisement"}
  This project tasked my team with creating a short public service announcement.  As such, we decided to satirize the unsanitary actions of the staff at the Commons dining hall.  This advertisement shows a sick worker at the pasta station haphazardly preparing food, where it eventually catches fire and triggers the fire alarm.  An announcer interrupts this scene to deliver a message about food safety.

  :::

  :::accordion-item{label="Project 3: Surrealist Newscast"}
  The goal of this project was to simulate a typical newscast, but make the news and delivery style unconventional. My team decided to produce 5 segments: breaking news, sports, weather, trends, and a political ad interruption with two insane presidential candidates.

## Digital Filmmaking

This was another introductory level class with a focus on film as opposed to television.

::accordion{type="multiple"}
  :::accordion-item{label="Project 1: Video Painting"}
  This project was simple, tell a story through one static shot. No editing or camera work was allowed, so everything had to be done live. My team decided to portray an evil admin office, where officials would take phone calls and direct their underlings to make life unpleasant for those at the bottom. Unfortunately, the recording of this was lost after the class, and none of my sound design work made it into the final cut.
  :::

  :::accordion-item{label="Project 2: Exquisite Corpse"}
  The goal of this project was to assemble a short film from segments that were assigned individually to each team member. The catch was that only the first or last shot of each segment could be shared among the team, and only to the member responsible for the previous or next scene. This project ended in disaster, as half the team could not speak English, and one of the team members did not do any work.
  :::

  :::accordion-item{label="Project 3: Video Collage"}
  This goal of this project was to remix existing footage into a 1-minute short film. Recording new footage was allowed, but it had to be kept to a minimum. Using stock footage and clips from YouTube, I synthesized a short story about the impacts of climate change and how presidents Joe Biden and Donald Trump have handled it.

  :iframe{crossorigin allow-full-screen="true" frame-border="0" referrer-policy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Yd30QNO28uA?si=Y6pKQb6TPaKt6usR" style="width: 100%; aspect-ratio: 16/9" title="YouTube video player"}
  :::

  :::accordion-item{label="Project 4: Make Your Own"}
  The final project in this class was open-ended, as long as every technique taught in the class was used (shot composition, audio mixing, lighting, editing). This project was also the first in a series of collaboration films I made with an increasingly close friend, Mary Hueston. For this project, we gathered a group of actors from the RPI Players and filmed a group therapy sketch, where each actor came to vent about their ridiculous problems with campus life. The session quickly devolves into accusations being hurled at the leader, who reveals a twist about his character.

## Intermediate Video

This course introduced new techniques, such as leading with audio and color correction.

::accordion{type="multiple"}
  :::accordion-item{label="Project 1: Video Portrait"}
  The goal of this project was to pick something or someone and tell a story about it. Naturally, to continue my streak of comedy films, I chose to make fun of my residence hall, the Blitman Residence Commons. For this project, I cast several current and former residents and had them sarcastically praise the "best" features of the building, and especially the campus shuttle that serves it.

  :iframe{crossorigin allow-full-screen="true" frame-border="0" referrer-policy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Ez_D9MBwlY8?si=i0nmS2Lo1FsNocAe" style="width: 100%; aspect-ratio: 16/9" title="YouTube video player"}
  :::

  :::accordion-item{label="Project 2: Scene Remake"}
  This project was a difficult one, as we were tasked with recreating a 5-minute scene from a movie or TV show shot-by-shot. To complicate things farther, Mary and I chose to recreate the "Apology Dance" scene from *Good Omens*, which had over 100 shots and an intricate set. The first part of this project was filmed in the Folsom Library, with some professional lighting equipment borrowed from the RPI Players to create a warm atmosphere for the bookshop. The second part was done in the studio on the green-screen, however, we were in a rush that resulted in shadows that were difficult to key out.

  :iframe{crossorigin allow-full-screen="true" frame-border="0" referrer-policy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Ox5cF9M7V5Y?si=JTb30JuYrt-PvICD" style="width: 100%; aspect-ratio: 16/9" title="YouTube video player"}
  :::

  :::accordion-item{label="Project 3: Music Video"}
  The final project was to create either a music video or a film that is led with sound. In yet another collaboration, Mary composed a parody to "Agony" from *Into the Woods*, and I wrote a screenplay depicting two failing students venting their frustrations to each other through song. The audio was recorded in the isolated booth in the studio, and this time, I made sure to light the green screen evenly to avoid shadows.

## Advanced Video: Media Studio

My final film class was an advanced course with a focus on studio production.  This class introduced more techniques, including front projection, rear projection, projection mapping, more advanced 3-camera shoots, and more detailed color correction.

::accordion{type="multiple"}
  :::accordion-item{label="Project 1: Documentary Film"}
  This project was an exercise in running a 3-camera shoot and editing the footage. The subject matter for this project was an experimental performance by a graduate student that needed documentation. The full class shot the footage and each student edited it to produce their own interpretation. For my interpretation, I switched around some of the cuts and added the Rensselaer Concert Choir's recording of *The Tuning Meditatation* by Pauline Oliveros as my soundtrack.

  :iframe{crossorigin allow-full-screen="true" frame-border="0" referrer-policy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/WHdmbKHceh8?si=brUDHrzvJl1hRJK1" style="width: 100%; aspect-ratio: 16/9" title="YouTube video player"}
  :::

  :::accordion-item{label="Project 2: Projections"}
  The goal of this project was to produce a short film that included at least one scene with front, rear, or mapped projection. While working on this project, the RPI Players were preparing for their production of *The Lightning Thief*, which Mary and I were both involved with. We recruited Joaquin Molinari, who was also involved with the show to produce a music video of "Drive", a song about the heroes traveling across the country. The joke with this is that the show's technical director was strictly opposed to using projections in the show, so we decided to have some fun with projections while we could. All of the scenery was rear projected onto a large screen, and most of the actors were the understudies of their respective roles in the RPI Players show. This project presented a number of color correction challenges, as the camera did not pick up the projections accurately.

  :iframe{crossorigin allow-full-screen="true" frame-border="0" referrer-policy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Xwo4iba4dxU?si=a2ztraYYr1xQIiON" style="width: 100%; aspect-ratio: 16/9" title="YouTube video player"}
  :::

  :::accordion-item{label="Project 3: Hybrid Film"}
  The final project in this class was to produce a short film that spanned multiple genres. After a little brainstorming, Mary, Joaquin, and I decided to blend the Noir Detective genre with science fiction. The result was a 20-minute film about a detective, played by Joaquin, who takes a lead about a murder and ends up in a sticky situation after a relative of the victim reveals a shocking secret. This film was directed by Mary and edited by me, and I'm not lying when I say that Joaquin wrote the entire script on an all-nighter and we didn't have to change it one bit!
