# Am I Enough of Me

![IMG-2305](/enough/IMG-2305.jpg)

In July 2025, I had the incredible opportunity to lighting design Cliff Watson's research project, *Am I Enough of Me*, in the EMPAC proscenium theater.  This project was a 6-part show that explored how different media could be used to tell a story about individuality in a dystopian corporate future.  The show opens with a brief meditation, asking the audience to think about themselves and their careers.  It then proceeds through a musical theatre number, an interactive AR scene, slam poetry, an AI-generated animated film, and a gameshow, concluding with a deeply reflective musical performance.  For more details, check out the event listing on [EMPAC's website](https://empac.rpi.edu/events/2025/am-i-enough-me-theatrical-production-and-research-project).

## Lighting Design

As the storyline of this project was somewhat disjointed, my lighting design primarily focused on creating a visual environment for each scene.  The musical theatre number had bright, neutral lighting with a color highlight for each character that transitioned to a gloomy look as they expressed their fears.  The AR scene featured a moving spotlight to augment the camera scanner that an actor was using to interact with the audience.  The poetry scene moves into a dull, haunting office space with pin spotlights to highlight each character as they recite their lines in sequence.  This is then contrasted by the bright and energetic lighting of the gameshow, along with color washes that represent a character's answer to each question.  Finally, the music performance features haze, pin specials, and a gloomy environment that brightens into daylight as the characters remember their true selves.

### Eos Showfile

The EMPAC theater features an 80'x40' stage, along with around 200 lighting fixtures, which would have made programming during tech week a hassle.  Instead, I built an Augment3d environment of the venue, placed and focused all of the lights on it, and programmed my cues using groups, palettes, and presets.  I then verified the accuracy of this 3D model once in the theater itself.
