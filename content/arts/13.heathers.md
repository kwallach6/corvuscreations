# Heathers

![The full cast of Heathers](/heathers/group-photo-complete.png)

*Heathers* was a major first for the RPI Players, in that the movement to produce this show overcame resistance from the Rensselaer Union regarding its content and, being the first show the RPI Players held in the proscenium theater at the Experimental Media and Performing Arts Center (EMPAC).  I joined this production as a lighting and sound assistant, helping to plan the lighting plot and cues and determine mic placement on actors.  This simple role quickly became messy, as I was tasked with programming the entire Eos showfile.

## The Beginning

*Heathers* was the second show affected by the RPI Playhouse flood on February 4, 2023.  Within a matter of weeks, the producer managed to secure a residency contract with EMPAC and Russell Sage College for the James L. Meader Little Theater.  After being invited as a lighting and sound assistant, the production preparation process proceeded normally without any major hiccups.  I was also told by the master electrician that he would like me to program the cues for this show, as I was the only member of the lighting department that had not yet had the opportunity.

## EMPAC

![A photo from the EMPAC version of Heathers](/heathers/heathers-empac.jpg)

Although I had already been working there for several months, EMPAC's "Nadawrep" lighting plot was something I had never controlled before.  On the first day of tech week, I spent around 4 hours channel-checking the plot and noting which fixtures were focused where.  I then recorded groups and submasters for similar fixtures to make programming easier.  From there, things only got worse.  Interpersonal conflict arose between me and the lighting designer, which delayed programming and created hostility throughout the entire crew.  Ultimately, this conflict caused the programming to not be complete until after the house opened on opening night, which resulted in a messy, unreadable showfile, and no practice for the followspot operators.  Thankfully, an organized stage manager and a demand from the master electrician that I run things got the show back on track, and it opened without any major problems.

**Download the [EMPAC showfile](/heathers/Heathers-EMPAC-2023-04-05-22-19-07.esf3d) and see for yourself!**

## Russell Sage College

![A photo from the Russell Sage College version of Heathers](/heathers/heathers-rsc.jpg)

For all the problems that happened during programming in EMPAC, this was nothing compared to the mess at Russell Sage College.  For one, Russell Sage College had no LED fixtures to provide the lighting designer he needed when making last-minute decisions.  Second, the venue was unfamiliar to the entire team, making everything harder to understand and work with.  The console also did not have encoders, which made programming the moving lights much more difficult.  On the other hand, programming went faster with far fewer fixtures, and was completed on time, though the showfile was still not the most readable.
