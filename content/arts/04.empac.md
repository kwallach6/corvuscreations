# EMPAC at RPI

![The EMPAC concert hall during the Shifting Center installation](/empac/volcano.jpg){width="4032" height="3024" :placeholder="[4032, 3024]" preload sizes="100vw lg:1024px"}

*Photo from the Fall 2023 "Shifting Center" exhibition.*

[The Curtis R. Priem Experimental Media and Performing Arts Center (EMPAC) at RPI](https://empac.rpi.edu) is a unique facility that curates emerging artists from around the world and takes advantage of RPI's technical infrastructure and expertise to help them develop their ideas. EMPAC is home to a world-famous concert hall, a state-of-the-art proscenium theater, two studios, and an advanced video and audio production suite. I had the privilege of working for Stage Technologies (lighting, rigging, and staging) for over 2 years.

## Featured Experiences

As a student worker, I worked on many of the residencies in all 4 venues.  My typical job involved hanging and focusing lights, assembling stage platforms, running cables, building lighting booms and trusses, relamping incandescent fixtures, rigging projection screens, and assisting with other departments as necessary.  One reality of being a student worker at an experimental arts center, however, is that I rarely ever saw the artists' completed works before I had to strike them.

### Concert Hall

The EMPAC concert hall is one of the most acoustically perfect venues in the world, where it is so reverberant that someone standing at the back of the balcony can understand a whisper from someone on stage, and it is so quiet when empty that anything you hear is from within your own body.  As someone who has experienced this firsthand, the silence is quite unnerving, and the resonance is wonderful for singers.  As the concert hall is mainly used for presentations, film screenings, recording sessions, and student concerts, much of my work in this venue was constantly relamping houselights, though there were two notable works I was involved with.

- [Shifting Center](https://empac.rpi.edu/events/2023/shifting-center-exhibition): An artist exhibited a large volcano sculpture and resin printed ravens that whistled when blown.  I helped build the volcano sculpture and focus a good number of lights for the installation.
- [Elemental View](https://empac.rpi.edu/events/2023/elemental-view): An artist used the hall's acoustic as the resonator of a giant string instrument by running strings across the stage and playing them like a violin or cello.  I helped focus lights into bars along each string and helped throw the 20,000 pounds of stage bricks needed to keep the strings taut!

### Theater

![The EMPAC proscenium theater](/empac/empac-theater.jpg){width="4032" height="3024" sizes="100vw lg:1024px"}

The proscenium theater was where most of the residencies I worked on took place.  It is just as quiet as the concert hall, but without the resonance.  The theater has an automated rigging system, a pit elevator, and a stage that sits less than a foot above the front row of seats, which artists take advantage of regularly to immerse the audience in their works.  Most of my work in this venue involved building lighting booms, hanging the 5 overstage electrics, dropping cables from the pinrails, flying soft goods, and occasionally driving the scissor lift to focus lights.  Some of the major works I was involved with are listed below.

- [Only Breath, Words](https://empac.rpi.edu/events/2022/only-breath-words): An immersive show where the artist projected messages across grids of lights and used the air rising from the HVAC vent under each seat to play wooden flutes.  I helped strike this show on my second day of work.
- [Paper Pianos](https://empac.rpi.edu/events/2023/paper-pianos): A concert documentary about the Afghan refugee crisis.  This work involved flying a grand piano and its pianist inside of a large metal cage.  I helped prepare the piano and cage for rigging, hung the lighting plot, and shadowed my coworker as he programmed the lights in real time over headset with the show's designer.
- [Phono Kinetic](https://empac.rpi.edu/events/2023/phono-kinetic): A music/dance performance where the artist wore a glove that interacted with the projection screen and a series of Chauvet ColorDash light fixtures placed throughout the house.  I helped hang the lighting plot and shadowed my coworkers a bit as they setup the show control integration.
- Shifting Center: The artist in the theater intentionally broke the acoustical isolation of the venue, blasting the audio from a film from the pit while bright flashes of lights accompanied it.  I helped hang the lighting plot for this as well.

### Studio 1

![The Studio 1 lighting grid](/empac/empac-studio-grid.jpg){width="4032" height="3024" sizes="100vw lg:1024px"}

*The EMPAC Studio 1 lighting grid.*

Studio 1 was where most of the residencies outside of the theater took place.  It features a tension wire grid, automated rigging, and a similar acoustic to the theater.  Most of my work in this venue involved hanging and focusing lights in the grid, relamping incandescent fixtures, flying projection screens manually, flying the trusses, building stage platforms and seating risers, and painting set pieces.  As most of what happens in Studio 1 rarely has a performance, it is impossible to associate much of my work to specific residencies.  Some of the notable residencies include the following.

- [Reverberating Light](https://empac.rpi.edu/research/2023/reverberating-light): An installation where mirrors projected light onto a series of screens.  The light would shake and change forms when visitors hit a gong.  I helped fly some of the projection screens for this installation.
- [Transtraterrestrial](https://empac.rpi.edu/events/2023/transtraterrestrial): An immersive experience involving a "spaceship" set.  I helped hang lights in both studios and the connecting hallway for this residency.

### Studio 2

Studio 2 is the smaller studio and has a 360 degree projection screen that is usually set up for some time each year.  Most residencies in this studio are presentations and seminars, therefore, most of my work involved relamping incandescent lights and focusing simple front washes for presenters.

## Other Experience

Outside of working on individual residencies, a lot of my work involved moving things around the building and performing routine maintenance on lighting fixtures.  This involved cleaning, checking for damaged parts, and "bench focusing" each ETC Source 4 fixture.  Be sure to check out two other projects I had the opportunity to work on in the theater outside of my student worker job.
